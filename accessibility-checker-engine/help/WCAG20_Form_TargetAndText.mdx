### Check that interacting with content does not initiate a change of context
User should be informed in advance when interacting with content causes a change of context

[IBM 3.2.2 On Input](http://www.ibm.com/able/guidelines/ci162/on_input.html) | [WCAG 2.1 technique 13](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Techniques/general/G13) | [WCAG 2.1 technique 80](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Techniques/general/G80)

<div id="locSnippet"></div>

### What to do

* Provide a submit button using an `<input>` element with a `type="submit"` attribute to intitate a change in context;
* OR, describe what will happen when interacting with content, before the change happens (see [WCAG 2.1 technique 13](https://www.w3.org/WAI/WCAG21/Techniques/general/G13).)


</Column>
<Column colLg={5} colMd={3} colSm={4} className="toolLeft">

### Who does this affect?

* Blind people using screen readers
* People with cognitive limitations
* Many older adults

### Why is this important?

If entering data in a form or selecting a form control causes be a change in context, users need to be informed. Interactive components with unpredictable behavior might change the user's context before they are ready, causing frustration or confusion.
